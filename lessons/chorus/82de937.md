# Lesson — Smoke a long-running background process via stdout-to-file + trap cleanup

## What happened
`monitor-inbox.sh` is a long-running poll loop that emits `inbox-change` sentinels on stdout. The smoke fixture creates a tmpdir, runs `monitor-inbox.sh` as a background process with stdout redirected to a file, uses real `touch` to advance mtime, then reads the file and asserts the sentinel appeared. A `trap` ensures any leftover background jobs are killed even when assertions fail mid-test.

## Transferable lesson
When testing a background process that emits events to stdout, never assert "stdout matched X" against the live stream — you'll race the producer. Pipe stdout into a file, sleep just enough for the producer to write, then `grep` the file. And: any test that backgrounds a process needs a `trap` cleanup; a failing assertion left in the middle of the test leaves a zombie process that pollutes the next run.

## Surface area
Next time you smoke a daemon, follow this skeleton: tmpdir fixture, `proc &; trap 'kill %1 2>/dev/null' EXIT`, redirect stdout to file, drive input, `grep` the file, then assert.
