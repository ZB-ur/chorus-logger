# Lesson — Acceptance evidence records every reboot's numbers (not just "passed") because the variance is itself the signal

## What happened
v0.5 acceptance recorded two reboots with full timings: Reboot 1 (pinger 32s / ponger 25s / round-trip 21s) and Reboot 2 (pinger 31s / ponger 45s / round-trip 44s). Zero manual Enter on both. The evidence file also documents the two BLOCKED logs (11 and 11b) and notes "honest reporting: 2 distinct BLOCK files written; never silently retried." All four boot-runs are visible.

## Transferable lesson
"Acceptance passed" is information-poor; "two reboots, here are the numbers" lets the next reader see variance, judge tail risk, and notice if a timing budget is borderline. Pair the numbers with explicit "we did NOT silently retry" — that one sentence is the trust-builder, because a harness that hides retries gives you green dashboards on top of brittle systems. Record failed attempts as BLOCKED logs in-tree; never collapse them.

## Surface area
Next time you write an acceptance/QA report, record N runs with their measurements and explicitly state your retry policy ("0 retries / failures committed as X" or "auto-retried 2x with reason Y") — never just "passed."
