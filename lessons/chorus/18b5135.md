# Lesson — When fixing a shape-mismatch bug, add a new test case modeled on captured real shape — don't just tweak the existing one

## What happened
The fix relaxed Stage 1's check from "last non-blank line ends with ❯" to "❯ anywhere in buffer." The settle-gate preserves the buffer-quiet robustness; the welcome banner is ASCII with no `❯` so banner-paint false-positives are impossible. Smoke gained a new Case 6 `realistic-repl` whose mock matches the actual buffer shape recorded in BLOCKED-task-11.md (❯ mid-buffer, UI chrome lines below) — rather than editing the original test that had certified the hypothesis.

## Transferable lesson
When a real-world shape diverges from your test fixture, do not "fix" the existing test — it's now historical evidence of what you thought was true. Add a new case modeled on the captured reality, leave the old case in place, and let the diff between them tell future readers the story. The framing also matters: this was a *detection bug* (heuristic location wrong) inside a *sound design*, not a design bug — that distinction kept the fix small.

## Surface area
Next time a smoke fixture is wrong, add a new fixture from real-shape capture, keep the original one labeled with its assumption, and frame the fix as detection vs. design before deciding scope.
