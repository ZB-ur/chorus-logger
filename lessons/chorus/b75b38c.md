# Lesson — Mark prereqs that only close under real-world conditions; don't pretend they can be force-closed by work-product

## What happened
The chorus-self prereq list closed 1 of 5 in v0.5 (#7 probe-for-prompt injector). Of the remaining four, one — "quota-watcher pause path exercised end-to-end at real >80% util" — was footnoted as "impractical to engineer" since real high utilization can't be staged on demand. Three others (cron-vs-tick design, tick atomicity, idle no-op) were left as engineering work for later releases.

## Transferable lesson
Roadmap prereqs come in two flavors: things you can build, and things you can only observe under real conditions. Conflating them lets the "observable-only" items hide behind work-product and accumulate unfounded confidence. Call them out explicitly with a footnote — "this closes when real X happens" — so a future reviewer asking "is chorus-self ready?" can see which boxes need code vs. patience.

## Surface area
Next time you write a roadmap/prereq list, separately label items as "engineered" vs "must be observed under load/scale/time" so you don't accidentally treat the latter as deliverables.
