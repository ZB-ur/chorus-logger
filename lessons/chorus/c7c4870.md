# Lesson — A "What this does NOT prove" list is a TODO list — close items inline, don't rewrite history

## What happened
The original v0 retrospective had a "What this does NOT prove" section. Cycle-4 closed one of those items (autonomous pause-write); the closure landed as an inline closed-post-cycle-4 marker on the original line plus a new section summarizing the audit chain, rather than as a rewrite of the original. Tag was deliberately NOT moved — v0 stays at acedff4; if the closure mattered enough to publish as a release it would have become v0.0.1.

## Transferable lesson
Treat retrospective gaps as living TODOs. When you close one, annotate the original line with a "closed by X" marker plus a new section that explains the closure — this preserves the evidence chain for anyone reading from the old tag. Reserve patch-version tags (v0.0.1) for doc-only follow-ups that change what a release "means"; don't move the original tag.

## Surface area
Next time you close a gap from an old retrospective or postmortem, leave the original wording intact, drop an inline marker, and decide explicitly whether the closure justifies a new patch tag.
