# Lesson — When you regex an observed log line, treat decorative glyphs and spacing as paint — skip them with `.*`, never match them literally

## What happened
Stage 2 regex matches `Monitor[[:space:]]+started.*task[[:space:]]+([a-z0-9]{8,})`. Cycle-5c condition 1 explicitly forbade including the literal middle-dot character that appears visually in the real log line between "started" and "task." Reason: a middle-dot is rendering output, not data — different terminals, encodings, or future log-format tweaks may emit something different in that slot. Use `.*` to skip the decorative span and match only the words/IDs you actually need.

## Transferable lesson
Logs designed for humans include separators (·, →, ┃, em-dashes, fancy spaces) that look like data when you stare at one captured sample. They are paint. A regex that pins them literally breaks the next time the log format is touched. Anchor your regex to the words and identifiers you actually consume; use `.*` (or `[^a-z]+`) across decorative spans. Treat every Unicode glyph in your match as a question: "is this load-bearing, or is it just rendering?"

## Surface area
Next time you transcribe a captured log line into a regex, mentally erase every non-ASCII glyph and replace each one with `.*` unless you're certain the writer commits to that exact character.
