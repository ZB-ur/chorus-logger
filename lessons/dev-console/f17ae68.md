# Lesson — When walking a project tree, prune noise dirs in-place during os.walk — never post-filter

## What happened
`latest_mtime` walks the directory and finds the most recent file mtime. The excluded-dir list is a `frozenset({".git", "node_modules", "__pycache__", ".claude", "dist", "build", ".next", "target", "vendor", ".venv", ".bundle", ".cache"})`. The prune happens via `dirnames[:] = [d for d in dirnames if d not in _EXCLUDED_DIRS]` — in-place mutation that `os.walk(topdown=True)` respects to skip recursion into the pruned dirs entirely.

## Transferable lesson
The wrong way to skip `node_modules` is to walk into it and filter results after — by then you've stat'd thousands of useless files and waited seconds. The right way: `os.walk(topdown=True, ...)` followed by `dirnames[:] = [...]` to mutate the list os.walk uses to decide its next descent. Two details: (a) it MUST be `dirnames[:] = ...`, not `dirnames = ...` — the latter rebinds the local, the former mutates the list os.walk holds; (b) use `frozenset` for the constant — O(1) lookup, immutable.

## Surface area
Next time you `os.walk` a project tree, immediately list its noise dirs as a `frozenset` and prune via `dirnames[:] = [...]` at the top of the loop — before any filename work.
